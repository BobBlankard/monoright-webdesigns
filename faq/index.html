Frequently asked questions

Straight answers for San Diego owners researching websites, paired with structured data that matches this visible content.

Do you use WordPress?

Our default brochure offer is a hand-coded static site for speed and a smaller attack surface. If your roadmap truly needs WordPress, say so in discovery. We will scope integrations honestly or refer you when that is the better fit.

How much does a small business website cost in San Diego?

Published anchors on our pricing page start at $2,000 lump sum (plus $25/mo hosting), $125/mo for the monthly package, and Shopify ecommerce from $4,500. Extra pages after five and blog setup use the fees listed there. Final quotes follow discovery and a written scope.

How long does a redesign take?

Most five-page brochure projects land in a few weeks once content and approvals keep pace. Larger content migrations, brand-new photography, or third-party booking integrations add calendar time and are estimated separately.

What pages does a local service business website need?

Typically home, services, proof or about, pricing or FAQs, and contact, plus location pages when you genuinely serve multiple cities with unique copy. Thin duplicate geo pages hurt more than they help.

Will my site be fast on phones?

We design mobile-first, keep JavaScript minimal, size images with discipline, and avoid lazy-loading the hero image that becomes your LCP element. Field data still varies by network; we remove obvious technical mistakes.

Who owns the domain and code?

Ownership should be explicit in your contract: registrar account, DNS, repository access, and whether you receive exports. We recommend clients own business-critical domains even when we help manage DNS.
